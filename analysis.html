Correlation Heatmap US

Customers in the United States appear to be more responsive to discounts, to the point that discount and unit price are almost equal factors in determining the quantity they wish to purchase. Like Canada, American consumers are more driven by Unit Price but to an even greater degree than their Canadian counterparts.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Correlation Heatmap Canada

For Canada, there appears to be a very weak correlation between discount and order quantity, sales, and profit, leading us to believe that applying discounts aren't very effective. Unit price seems to be more of a driving factor in determining consumer decisions.

Our Analysis

Returns

Lost Sales

The percent of lost sales from returns varies more widely, with the lowest being 5.9% in 2013 to 12.1% in 2010. There are certain individuals who make frequent returns. Darren Bud has made 16 returns in 3 years. 16 people have made at least ten returns in three years.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Top Returns

The most commonly returned item is Staples (17). However, the top 20 contain a fair mix of Office Supplies, Furniture, and Technology. There were 1,672 returns: 934 (55.86%) in Office Supplies, 374 (22.37%) in Technology, and 364 (22.37%) in Furniture.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Rate of Returns

About 8.1-10.9% of the orders are being returned per year and remains relatively consistent throughout all six years in spite of very large fluctuations in sales. The average return on a single transaction is $1,097.71. This is skewed, because there have been several big-ticket items returned.
