The Data

Learn About the Data

18300+ Rows of Data

Transactional data was provided by Superstore in CSV format with invoices, customers, and return data from U.S. and Canada. Data represents the sales of a Superstore that primarily sells office supplies, technology, & furniture. There are 4 segments of customers, the consumer, corporate, home office, and small business which make up the following percentage of their customer base.

23%

Corporate

Data Limitations

  • There is limited data beyond basic sales and customer data.
  • States with fewer sales skewed buying portfolio (ex. Wyoming only had only one sale in six years, and it was Home Office, so Wyoming shows 100% of sales in Home Office).
  • On returns, it does not show why items were returned, so that limits any recommendations beyond improving quality control (ensuring that the products from our vendors meet quality standards) and customer service.
  • There is very little vendor data. Did any of the vendors go out of business? Would the company be able to negotiate better prices from our vendors in order to increase Superstore’s profit margin? Did a rival company, like Amazon, take more market share in 2013-2014?
  • There was a very large drop in sales in 2013 and 2014. What factors accounted for that?
  • The Superstore Regional Offices in Canada and the United States classify their Product Sub-categories differently (for example: “Binders & Binder Accessories” in Canada can be two categories—“Binders” and “Accessories”—in the United States. The Query Crew recommends that Superstore unifies its Inventory Classification system to enable more precise analysis on its Product Pricing Models.
